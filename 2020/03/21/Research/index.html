Research Interest

Using advanced technology to solve some medical problems, is the motivation for my research forever. However, we are not the professional medical researchers or doctors, what should we do to help patents and doctors?

As I was very fascinated by Robot, so I unhesitatingly joined Water Robot Team, a best student team in my university, when I was an entering freshmen. With my team members, we started pursuing a project in the emerging interdisciplinary field of Robot and Health Medical. We collaborated on intelligent wheelchair controlled by brain wave to help patients, who have severe movement disorders, communicate by using eye blink detected from brain waves and same can be used control the wheel chair. My thesis project was about mechanical structure design of rehabilitation robot to increase the ease of activities in the daily lives of patients..

Now，I am in Prof. Zhonglin Wang’s Group, as a muster student, to research on nanogenerators, self-powered nanosystems and bio-inspired intelligent materials, and I witness the nanogenerator has been applied from laboratory to commercial field to solve the power needs for small electronics or even medical problems. Meanwhile, I know more about other applications of nanogenerator like in , by regularly communicating with my friends who are in other research group. Recently, I am engaging in a project about using nanogenetor to give the paralysed new tactile sense.

In future, my research effort will concentrate on to more valuable, interesting, challenging and interdisciplinary research about wearable electronics based Nanogenerator to solve some medical problems. If possible , I also want to develop medical miniature robots that work in vivo and in vitro, which can be potentially deployed in minimally invasive diagnosis and therapeutics. Issues such as biocompatibility and biodegradability will be taken into consideration in the design of the medical robots. When deployed inside human bodies, the feedback information of the robots is limited and obscured, in which scenarios accurate and reliable actuation and control of these robots are challenging and will be investigated in my following research.
